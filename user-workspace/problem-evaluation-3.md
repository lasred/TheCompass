# Problem Evaluation: Time Tracking & Billing Automation for Accounting Firms

**Problem:** Accounting firms billing by the hour lose 10-20% of billable hours due to poor time tracking, leading to write-offs and delayed billing.

**Industry:** Small Accounting & CPA Firms

**Business type:** Solo to 20-person accounting/tax firms that bill hourly (vs flat fee)

**Evaluated:** November 11, 2025

**Skill used:** problem-identifier

---

## Evaluation Against 4 Criteria

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Criterion 1: Manual Process Hell

**Status:** PARTIAL - Semi-manual, but varies widely

**Details:** Current process for firms billing hourly:
1. Start timer when working on client engagement (or forget to start)
2. Stop timer when done (or forget to stop)
3. End of week: Reconstruct time if forgot to track
4. Review time entries for accuracy
5. Manually create invoices from time sheets
6. Send invoices to clients
7. Track which invoices are paid vs unpaid

**Current tools used:**
- **QuickBooks Time** (some firms) - Clunky but functional
- **Karbon** (some firms) - Built-in time tracking
- **Excel spreadsheets** (some firms) - Manual time logs
- **Memory/honor system** (some firms) - "I think I spent 8 hours on this"

**Assessment:**
This is LESS manual than document collection or workflow visibility because:
- Many firms already use time tracking software (QuickBooks Time, Karbon, etc.)
- The manual part is often "remembering to track" not "the tracking itself"
- Invoicing CAN be automated with existing tools

The manual hell exists, but it's more about **friction** (too many clicks, forget to start timer) than pure "we use paper and spreadsheets."

Some firms ARE purely manual (Excel time logs), but many have tools‚Äîthey just don't use them well.

**This is the weakest criterion for this problem.** It's not that they lack tools; it's that existing tools are clunky or they don't have discipline to use them.

**Evidence from research:**
- "Accountants hate tracking time (too many clicks)"
- "Forget to start timer, reconstruct time at end of week (inaccurate)"
- "Manual invoice creation from time sheets"

But also:
- QuickBooks Time exists ($8-$20/user/month)
- Karbon includes time tracking
- Tools exist, just not great

### ‚úÖ Criterion 2: High-Frequency Repetition

**Status:** YES - Constant, multiple times daily

**Frequency:**
- **Per staff member:** Start/stop timer 5-10 times per day
- **Per firm:** If 5 staff members √ó 5 timers/day = 25 time entries per day
- **Monthly:** 25/day √ó 20 workdays = 500 time entries per month
- **Annual:** 500 √ó 12 = 6,000 time entries per year

**Invoicing frequency:**
- **Per client:** Monthly or per-engagement billing
- **Per firm:** If 30 clients, 30 invoices per month
- **Annual:** 360 invoices per year

**Assessment:**
This easily exceeds the 10+ times per month threshold. Time tracking happens MULTIPLE TIMES PER DAY for every staff member. Invoicing happens dozens of times per month.

The frequency is extremely high, making this a strong criterion.

**Evidence from research:**
- "Every engagement, every month"
- Hourly billing firms track time constantly (daily, multiple times per day)
- Invoice creation happens monthly or more often

### ‚úÖ Criterion 3: Compliance or Revenue-Critical

**Status:** YES - Strongly revenue-critical

**Type:** Revenue-Critical (direct)

**Revenue aspect:**
- **Unbilled hours = lost revenue:** 10-20% of hours go unbilled due to poor tracking = direct revenue loss
- **Write-offs = lost profit:** Firm works 100 hours, only bills 85 = 15% revenue lost
- **Delayed billing = cash flow problems:** Takes 2-5 days to create invoices manually = delayed payment
- **Client disputes:** Surprise bills lead to negotiation, discounts, write-offs

**Direct revenue impact calculation:**
- Firm with $500K revenue, 15% write-off rate = **$75,000 in unbilled time annually**
- Recapturing even 50% of write-offs = **$37,500/year** direct revenue gain

**Assessment:**
This is STRONGLY revenue-critical. It directly affects the firm's ability to capture revenue for work performed. Every unbilled hour is cash left on the table.

This is one of the strongest revenue-critical problems you can have‚Äîit's a direct leak in the revenue pipe.

**Compliance aspect:**
- Not compliance-mandated (no law requiring time tracking)
- Indirectly related to client billing compliance (accurate billing)

**Evidence from research:**
- "20-30% of billable hours go unbilled due to poor tracking"
- "Average firm writes off 10-15% of time = lost revenue"
- "Delayed billing = delayed cash flow"
- "CPA firms often struggle to run their own businesses effectively, particularly in pricing, billing, and collections"

### ‚ùå Criterion 4: Terrible Existing Software

**Status:** NO - Many existing solutions already exist

**Current options:**

1. **QuickBooks Time** ($8-$20/user/month)
   - **Works:** Simple time tracking, integrates with QuickBooks
   - **Downside:** Generic (not accounting-specific), clunky UX
   - **But:** Functional and affordable

2. **Karbon** ($89/user/month)
   - **Works:** Built-in time tracking for accounting firms
   - **Downside:** Expensive overall
   - **But:** Does the job well

3. **TimeSolv** ($39-$69/user/month)
   - **Works:** Designed for legal and accounting
   - **Downside:** Dated UX
   - **But:** Established solution

4. **Toggl Track** ($10-$20/user/month)
   - **Works:** Great UX, simple timer
   - **Downside:** Generic, not accounting-specific
   - **But:** Widely used and loved

5. **Harvest** ($12-$16/user/month)
   - **Works:** Time tracking + invoicing
   - **Downside:** Generic
   - **But:** Solid product

**Assessment:**
This is the WEAKEST criterion. The market is CROWDED with time tracking and billing solutions. There are dozens of options at various price points, from $8/month to $89/month.

The problem isn't that software doesn't exist‚Äîit's that:
- Accountants don't like ANY time tracking (it's tedious)
- Existing tools require too many clicks
- People forget to track

**The real problem is behavior/discipline, not lack of software.**

You could build "AI-assisted time tracking" or "one-click billing," but you're competing with:
- Established players (QuickBooks, Karbon)
- Well-funded startups (Toggl, Harvest)
- Accounting-specific tools (TimeSolv)

**Gap in market:**
Arguably, "AI-assisted time tracking" (auto-suggest time based on calendar/email) doesn't exist. But this is a feature, not a standalone product. You'd need a compelling differentiation beyond "easier to use."

**Evidence from research:**
- "QuickBooks Time ($8-$20/user/month) - Generic, not accounting-specific"
- "Karbon (includes time tracking, but expensive overall)"
- "TimeSolv ($39-$69/user/month) - Legal/accounting focus, dated UX"

But also:
- Many options exist and work
- Competition is HIGH

## Value Calculation

### Revenue Recapture from Better Time Tracking:

**Current situation:**
- Firm with $500,000 annual revenue
- 15% write-off rate (unbilled hours) = $75,000 lost annually
- Time tracking friction causes this loss

**With better time tracking:**
- Recapture 50% of write-offs = $37,500 additional revenue
- Or recapture 30% = $22,500 additional revenue

**Conservative annual value:** $22,500-$37,500/year

---

### Time Saved from Faster Billing:

**Current process:**
- End of month: Gather time sheets from all staff (1 hour)
- Review for accuracy (1 hour)
- Create invoices manually (30 min per invoice √ó 30 clients = 15 hours)
- Total: 17 hours per month

**With automation:**
- Time auto-tracked and categorized
- One-click invoice generation
- Total: 2 hours per month

**Time saved:** 15 hours/month = 180 hours/year

**Hourly cost:** $50/hour (admin or junior staff doing this work)

**Annual value (time savings):** 180 hours √ó $50 = **$9,000/year**

---

### Cash Flow Improvement:

**Current:** Invoices sent 5 days after month-end (due to manual process)

**With automation:** Invoices sent 1 day after month-end

**Improvement:** Get paid 4 days faster

**Value:** Hard to quantify, but cash flow improvement is real (faster payment cycles)

---

### Total Annual Value:

- **Revenue recapture:** $22,500-$37,500/year
- **Time savings:** $9,000/year
- **Cash flow:** Unmeasured but valuable

**Conservative total: $30,000-$45,000/year**

## Why You Should Probably Skip This

**The core issue:**
This isn't a "no software exists" problem. It's a "people don't want to track time" problem.

Even the BEST time tracking software (Toggl, Harvest) can't make people love tracking time. Accountants will always find it tedious.

Your competition isn't just other software‚Äîit's human behavior.

**And the real killer:**
