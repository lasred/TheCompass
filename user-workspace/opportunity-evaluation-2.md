# Opportunity Evaluation: Tax Season Capacity Planning & Client Prioritization

**Problem:** Accounting firms doing 100+ tax returns per season can't manage workload, prioritize clients effectively, or balance team capacity, leading to burnout, missed deadlines, and revenue inefficiency.

**Industry:** Small to Mid-Size Accounting & CPA Firms (Tax-focused)

**Business type:** 2-20 person accounting/tax firms doing 100-500+ tax returns per season

**Evaluated:** November 15, 2025

**Skill used:** problem-identifier

**Friend's feedback:** "Friend likes this. Something there would sell for sure. My friend definitely emphasizes the pain of the tax season."

## Evaluation Against 4 Criteria

### ‚úÖ Criterion 1: Manual Process Hell

**Status:** YES - Highly manual, chaotic process

**Details:** Current process for most firms during tax season:
1. Receive tax return requests from 100-200 clients in Jan-Mar
2. Try to schedule/prioritize using Excel spreadsheet or gut feel
3. Assign returns to staff members based on "who's available right now"
4. No visibility into who's overloaded (working 70 hours/week) vs who has capacity (working 40 hours)
5. Clients call asking "when will my return be ready?" - no clear answer
6. High-value clients ($2K returns) get same priority as simple $300 returns
7. Work piles up in March as everyone procrastinates until last minute
8. Constant firefighting: "Who can take the Johnson return? It's due tomorrow!"
9. Manual tracking of completion status across 100-200 returns
10. Staff burnout leads to errors, missed deadlines, turnover mid-season

**Current tools used:**
- Excel spreadsheets to track returns
- Whiteboard with client names
- Memory ("I think Sarah has capacity this week")
- Email/Slack for coordination
- Weekly panic meetings to redistribute work

**Assessment:**
This is coordination and capacity planning hell during the most stressful time of year. The manual nature creates:
- Uneven workload (some staff crushing it, others overwhelmed)
- Poor prioritization (squeaky wheel gets the grease, not strategic clients)
- No proactive planning (all reactive firefighting)
- Burnout and turnover

**Evidence from research:**
- "Scheduling chaos during tax season"
- "Reddit threads full of accountants describing scheduling nightmares"
- "Can't balance workload across team"
- "Some staff working 70 hours/week, others 40"
- "All clients want their taxes done in March (last minute)"
