**Industry:** Accounting Firms (selling to their small business clients)

**Business type:** Tool for accountants to provide to their small business clients (restaurants, retail, services, etc.)

## Evaluation Against 4 Criteria

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Criterion 1: Manual Process Hell
**Status:** YES for clients, MAYBE for accountants

**Current situation:**
- Small businesses check bank balance to make decisions ("Do I have enough to buy inventory?")
- No forward-looking cash flow projection
- Accountants provide monthly/quarterly financials (backward-looking, not predictive)
- Businesses run out of cash unexpectedly
- Manual Excel forecasting if they do it at all

**Assessment:** Pain exists but it's the CLIENT'S pain, not the accountant's direct pain. Harder to sell.

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Criterion 2: High-Frequency Repetition  
**Status:** MIXED

**Frequency:**
- Clients need cash flow info daily/weekly
- Accountants deal with client cash flow questions monthly
- But is the accountant ACTIVELY looking for a solution? Or do they just refer clients to QuickBooks?

**Assessment:** Frequency exists but demand from accountants (the buyer) is unclear.

---

### ‚úÖ Criterion 3: Compliance or Revenue-Critical
**Status:** YES - Revenue-critical for accounting firms

**Why firms care:**
- **Client retention:** If client's business fails due to cash flow, firm loses that client
- **Value-add service:** Cash flow tools let accountants charge more (advisory vs just tax prep)
- **Competitive differentiation:** "We give you real-time cash flow insights" vs competitors

**Assessment:** This is a value-add tool, not a core pain solver for accountants.

---

### ‚ùå Criterion 4: Terrible Existing Software
**Status:** NO - Many solutions already exist

**Existing options:**
- **QuickBooks Cash Flow Planner** (built-in, free with QBO)
- **Float** (cash flow forecasting, $49-149/month)  
- **Pulse** (cash flow management, $29-89/month)
- **Fluidly** (cash flow forecasting + lending)
- **Dryrun** (visual cash flow forecasting)
- **Many others** in this space

**Assessment:** This is a CROWDED market. Hard to differentiate.

## Value Calculation

**For accounting firm:**
- Retain 1-2 clients who would have failed = $5K-$20K annual revenue saved
- Upsell cash flow advisory service = $500-2,000/month per client
- But requires selling B2B2C (firm ‚Üí client), which is complex

**For small business client:**
- Avoid cash flow crisis = business survival
- Better decision-making = more profitable
- But SMBs already have cheap/free options (QuickBooks, Float at $49/month)

---

## Suggested Pricing

**B2B (Sell to accounting firms):**
- $49-99/month per firm (white-label for their clients)
- $10-20/month per client the firm adds

**B2C (Sell direct to small businesses):**
- $29-49/month for the business owner
- Competes with Float ($49), Pulse ($29), and QuickBooks (free)
