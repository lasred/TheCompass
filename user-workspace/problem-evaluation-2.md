# Problem Evaluation: Workflow Visibility & Engagement Tracking for Accounting Firms

**Problem:** Accounting firms with 2+ employees can't see what's happening across the firm‚Äîwhich engagements are on track, which are at risk of missing deadlines, and who has capacity to take on more work.

**Industry:** Small Accounting & CPA Firms

**Business type:** 2-20 person accounting/tax firms with 50+ active engagements

**Evaluated:** November 11, 2025

**Skill used:** problem-identifier

---

## Evaluation Against 4 Criteria

### ‚úÖ Criterion 1: Manual Process Hell

**Status:** YES - Highly manual, scattered process

**Details:** Current process for most firms:
1. Managing partner asks staff: "Where are we on the Johnson tax return?"
2. Staff member checks their email, files, memory
3. Responds verbally or via Slack/email
4. Partner writes it down or remembers it
5. Repeat for every single engagement
6. Weekly status meetings to check on everything (3-5 hours/week)
7. Tracking in spreadsheets, whiteboards, or just memory
8. No real-time view of team capacity or workload balance

**Current tools used:**
- Excel spreadsheets with engagement lists
- Whiteboards in office
- Email threads
- Slack/Teams messages
- Weekly status meetings
- Memory

**Assessment:**
This is manual process hell, but one level removed from pure data entry. It's "coordination hell"‚Äîthe manual work is asking people, tracking responses, updating spreadsheets, and holding meetings. The mental overhead of "I don't know what's happening" creates constant stress.

Not as tactile as "entering data into spreadsheets" but still qualifies as manual because there's no automated system providing real-time visibility.

**Evidence from research:**
- "Missing deadlines without knowing it"
- "Losing track of tasks as workload grows"
- "No visibility into ongoing work"
- "Making file notes is the first habit to slip when busy"
- 55.5% of firm owners experience workflow inefficiencies

---

### ‚úÖ Criterion 2: High-Frequency Repetition

**Status:** YES - Constant, daily problem

**Frequency:**
- **Daily:** Managing partner checks status of 5-10 engagements
- **Weekly:** Status meetings with team (1-2 meetings per week)
- **Per engagement:** Status checks happen 3-5 times throughout lifecycle
- **Annual:** If firm has 200 engagements/year √ó 4 status checks each = 800 status checks/year

**Detailed frequency breakdown:**
- Firm with 50 active engagements at any time
- Partner checks on 5-10 per day = 25-50 checks per week = 100-200 per month
- Plus weekly status meetings = 4 per month √ó 1-2 hours each = 4-8 hours/month in meetings

**Assessment:**
This easily exceeds the 10+ times per month threshold. In fact, it happens multiple times PER DAY. Every time a managing partner wonders "Are we going to miss a deadline?" or "Who has capacity?" they're experiencing this pain.

The problem is continuous during busy season and frequent year-round.

**Evidence from research:**
- "Continuous problem during busy season, tax season"
- "55.5% of firm owners experience workflow inefficiencies"
- Status meetings happen weekly or more often
- Daily question: "Where are we on X engagement?"

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Criterion 3: Compliance or Revenue-Critical

**Status:** PARTIAL - More revenue-critical than compliance-critical

**Type:** Revenue-Critical (indirect)

**Revenue aspect:**
- **Missed deadlines = lost clients:** If engagement falls through cracks, client may leave ($5K-$50K lifetime value)
- **Inefficient utilization = lost revenue:** If one staff member is overloaded while another has capacity, firm loses billable hours
- **Rush fees avoided:** Better visibility prevents last-minute scrambles that require rush work (which often isn't billable)
- **Client retention:** Better deadline management = happier clients = retention

**Compliance aspect:**
- **Indirect:** Missing a tax deadline IS compliance-critical, but the workflow tracking itself is not directly mandated by law
- Workflow visibility is a TOOL to prevent compliance issues, not itself a compliance requirement

**Assessment:**
This is revenue-critical, but it's one step removed from direct revenue impact.

It's not like document collection where "no docs = can't bill." Instead, it's "bad workflow visibility = missed deadlines, inefficiency, lost clients."

The impact is real and measurable, but it's indirect. You have to explain the chain: "Bad visibility ‚Üí missed deadline ‚Üí unhappy client ‚Üí lost client ‚Üí lost revenue."

This is the weakest of the 4 criteria for this problem. It DOES meet the bar (revenue-critical), but not as strongly as document collection.

**Evidence from research:**
- "Missed deadlines, unhappy clients"
- "Leads to rushed work, missed deadlines, unhappy clients"
- "Burnout, missed deadlines, uneven utilization"
- Impact on revenue is real but indirect

### ‚úÖ Criterion 4: Terrible Existing Software

**Status:** YES - Existing solutions don't fit solo/small firms

**Current options and why they fail:**

1. **Karbon** ($89/user/month)
   - **Problem:** Too expensive for small firms
   - **Cost:** 10-person firm = $10,680/year
   - **Problem:** Steep learning curve, takes weeks to set up
   - **Gap:** Overkill for firms under 10 people

2. **TaxDome** ($50-$300/month depending on features)
   - **Problem:** Overcomplicated, features feel underdeveloped
   - **Problem:** Too many features, hard to learn
   - **Gap:** Tries to do everything, doesn't excel at workflow visibility

3. **Jetpack Workflow** ($30-$59/month)
   - **Problem:** Too basic‚Äîlacks real-time capacity view
   - **Problem:** No team capacity dashboard
   - **Gap:** Shows tasks but not who's overloaded vs has capacity

4. **Practice management tools (generic)**
   - **Problem:** Built for mid-size firms (20-50 people)
   - **Problem:** Expensive and complex
   - **Gap:** Small firms (2-10 people) underserved

5. **What most firms actually use:** Excel + meetings
   - **Problem:** Spreadsheets go stale immediately
   - **Problem:** Status meetings waste 3-5 hours/week
   - **Problem:** No real-time view

**Assessment:**
The market has options, but they're either:
- Too expensive for 2-10 person firms
- Too complex (designed for larger firms)
- Too basic (missing real-time capacity view)

The gap: A simple, affordable, real-time workflow dashboard specifically for 2-10 person accounting firms with a focus on:
- Engagement status at a glance
- Team capacity view (who's overloaded, who has room)
- Deadline alerts
- Under $200/month

This gap exists but is narrower than document collection. There ARE tools (Karbon, TaxDome), they're just not ideal for small firms.

**Evidence from research:**
- "Current tools either too complex (Karbon) or too basic (Jetpack Workflow)"
- "Missing: Real-time team capacity view"
- "Karbon: $89/user/month = $1,068/year solo, $10,680 for 10 users - Too expensive"

## Value Calculation

### Time Saved:

**Current manual process (per week):**
- Daily status checks: 30 min/day √ó 5 days = 2.5 hours/week
- Weekly status meeting: 2 hours/week
- Ad-hoc "where are we on X?" questions: 1 hour/week
- **Total time per week:** 5.5 hours

**With automated dashboard:**
- Daily dashboard check: 5 min/day √ó 5 days = 25 min/week (0.4 hours)
- Weekly status meeting: Eliminated or reduced to 30 min
- Ad-hoc questions: Mostly eliminated (people check dashboard)
- **Total time per week:** 1 hour

**Time saved per week:** 4.5 hours

**Time saved:**
- 4.5 hours/week √ó 50 weeks/year = 225 hours/year
