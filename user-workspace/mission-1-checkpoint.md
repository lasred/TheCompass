### Problem #1: Client Document Collection üèÜ **(PURSUING THIS ONE)**

**Description:**
Accounting firms waste 10-20 hours/month chasing clients for documents (W-2s, 1099s, receipts, bank statements) via email, phone calls, and manual follow-ups. Clients send docs via random channels (email, text, physical mail), firms track status in spreadsheets or memory, causing chaos especially during tax season.

**The 4 Criteria:**
- ‚úÖ **Manual Process Hell:** Email follow-ups, phone calls, spreadsheet tracking, manual file organization. Pure chaos.
- ‚úÖ **High-Frequency:** 100-300 document collection cycles per year per firm. Every client, every engagement.
- ‚úÖ **Compliance/Revenue-Critical:** BOTH. Tax deadlines are legally mandated (compliance). Can't bill until docs arrive (revenue). Missed deadline = penalties + lost client.
- ‚úÖ **Terrible Software:** Existing options are too expensive (Liscio $480-$960/year), too generic (ContentSnare), or firms just use email. Password fatigue prevents portal adoption.

**Value Calculation:**
- Time per client: 31 minutes (follow-ups, tracking, organizing)
- Frequency: 100 collection cycles/year (firm with 50 clients √ó 2 requests each)
- Hourly cost: $40/hour (staff accountant)
- **Annual time savings:** 51.7 hours √ó $40 = $2,068
- **Plus deadline prevention:** $500-$10,000/year (avoid one missed deadline)
- **Plus client retention:** $5,000-$50,000/year (retain clients who'd leave due to poor service)
- **Total annual value:** $15,000-$30,000

**Potential pricing:** $99-$199/month ($1,188-$2,388/year) = **12-20x ROI for client**
