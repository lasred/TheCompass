- **Focus your research:** Stop wasting time on businesses that can't afford you

- **Find the right prospects:** Know exactly who to target

## The Client Avatar Framework

### Section 1: Company Profile

**Questions to ask:**
1. "What industry are you targeting?"
2. "What size companies?" (employees and/or annual revenue)
3. "Any specific geography?"
4. "What's their business model?"

**Good answers are specific:**
- ‚ùå "Small businesses"
- ‚úÖ "Fire inspection companies with 5-20 employees, $500K-$3M annual revenue, operating in California"

**Verification required:**
- Search for industry size data
- Cite sources for revenue ranges
- Mark as estimates if no data found

### Section 2: Decision Maker

**Questions to ask:**
1. "Who makes software purchasing decisions?"
2. "What's their typical title?"
3. "What are their daily responsibilities?"
4. "What keeps them up at night?"

**Verification required:**
- Search job postings for actual titles
- Check LinkedIn profiles
- Find forum discussions about decision makers
- Don't assume - verify

### Section 3: Current Situation

**Questions to ask:**
1. "How are they currently handling this problem?"
2. "Why does their current solution suck?"
3. "What have they tried before?"
4. "What's the annual cost of NOT solving this?"

**Verification required:**
- Find forum posts showing current tools
- Link to complaints about current solutions
- Show value calculation with sources

### Section 4: Budget & Buying Capacity

**Questions to ask:**
1. "What's their annual revenue range?"
2. "Do they currently spend money on software?"
3. "What's a reasonable investment for them?"
4. "How do they typically make purchasing decisions?"

**Verification required:**
- Show ROI calculation
- Cite sources for revenue data
- Explain budget estimate methodology
- Clearly label what's estimated

### Section 5: Success Metrics

**Questions to ask:**
1. "What results matter most to them?"
2. "How do they measure success?"
3. "What would make this a no-brainer investment?"

### Section 6: Messaging & Positioning

Based on everything above, define:
1. **Lead with:** Their #1 pain point
2. **Emphasize:** The specific outcome they care about
3. **Differentiate:** Why you're better than alternatives
4. **Proof:** Similar businesses you've helped

- Understand THEIR authority level
