# Target Industries (Tier 1)

These industries are proven to have boring, valuable B2B problems.

## The List

### 1. Fire Safety & Inspection
- **Why it works:** Manual compliance forms, frequent inspections, state-required reporting
- **Common problems:** AES forms, scheduling, client portals, compliance tracking
- **Market size:** 3,000-4,000 companies in California, 15,000-20,000 nationally

### 2. HVAC Inspection & Maintenance
- **Why it works:** High-frequency service visits, compliance documentation, scheduling chaos
- **Common problems:** Work order management, compliance reports, scheduling, invoicing
- **Market size:** Massive - every city has dozens of HVAC companies

### 3. Building Code Compliance
- **Why it works:** Government mandated, high-frequency, terrible existing software
- **Common problems:** Inspection tracking, compliance documentation, client communication
- **Market size:** Thousands of inspectors and compliance companies

### 4. Food Safety Inspection
- **Why it works:** Health department requirements, frequent inspections, critical for restaurant operations
- **Common problems:** Inspection reports, compliance tracking, scheduling
- **Market size:** Every city has food safety inspectors

### 5. Property Management
- **Why it works:** Recurring tasks, compliance across many units, revenue-critical
- **Common problems:** Maintenance scheduling, compliance documentation, tenant portals
- **Market size:** Massive - millions of rental units need management

### 6. Contractors & Trades
- **Why it works:** High-frequency jobs, manual quoting/invoicing, scheduling nightmares
- **Common problems:** Job management, scheduling, invoicing, client communication
- **Market size:** Plumbers, electricians, carpenters - every trade

### 7. Elevator Inspection & Maintenance
- **Why it works:** Government required, high-frequency, outdated software
- **Common problems:** Inspection tracking, compliance reports, scheduling
- **Market size:** Every commercial building needs elevator maintenance

### 8. Landscaping & Recurring Services
- **Why it works:** High-frequency visits, seasonal scheduling, invoicing complexity
- **Common problems:** Route optimization, scheduling, invoicing, client portals
- **Market size:** Massive - every business and home needs landscaping

## Why These Industries Work

### Common Characteristics:

**1. High-Frequency Operations**
- They do the same tasks 10+ times per month (minimum)
- Often 50-100+ times per week
- More frequency = more value = easier to justify price

**2. Compliance-Heavy**
- Government regulations require documentation
- They MUST do this or they can't operate
- Compliance = non-negotiable = they'll pay

**3. Terrible/Nonexistent Software**
- Generic software doesn't fit their specific workflow
- Existing tools are outdated (2008-era) or too expensive
- They're using spreadsheets and paper

**4. Fragmented Markets**
- Thousands of small businesses (5-50 employees)
- Not dominated by giant corporations
- Small businesses need custom solutions but can't afford enterprise software

## How to Research These Industries

### Online Research (No calls yet):

**1. Google Search**
- "[Industry] + [City] + companies"
- Example: "fire inspection Los Angeles companies"
- Find 10-15 businesses to research

**2. Company Websites**
- Look at their services
- Read "About Us" - understand their operations
- Check reviews - see what clients complain about

**3. Reddit**
- r/[industry] subreddits
- Search for pain points, software complaints
- Example: r/HVAC, r/Locksmith, r/PropertyManagement

**4. Industry Forums**
- ContractorTalk.com
- HVAC-Talk.com
- Industry-specific forums
- Look for software complaints and manual process discussions

**5. Review Sites**
- Yelp reviews of businesses in the industry
- Google reviews
- Look for: "They were late", "Poor communication", "Invoicing issues"
- These hint at operational problems

**6. Software Review Sites**
- Capterra, G2, Software Advice
- Search for [Industry] software
- Read 1-star and 2-star reviews
- Common complaints = opportunity

## Picking Your Industry

### Don't overthink it:

**Just pick one.** You can change later if needed.

**Good reasons to pick an industry:**
- You know someone in that industry
- You've worked in that industry before
- It's local to you (easier to find businesses)
- You find it interesting

**Bad reasons NOT to pick an industry:**
- "Too competitive" (there isn't competition yet)
- "Too niche" (niche is good)
- "I don't know anything about it" (you'll learn)

### Focus:

Pick exactly 3 industries for Week 1.

Deep research across three niches gives you comparison data to make the right choice.

You're learning to spot valuable problems. Testing across three industries prevents tunnel vision and gives you backup options.
