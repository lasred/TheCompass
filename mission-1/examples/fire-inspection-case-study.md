# Case Study: Fire Inspection Company

Real case study from one of Zane's clients. Real numbers. Real problem. Real solution.

---

## The Client

**Company:** Regional fire inspection company
**Industry:** Fire inspection services
**Size:** 5 inspectors
**Location:** California
**Annual revenue:** ~$1.5M

---

## The Problem

### Manual Process Hell ‚úÖ

After every fire inspection, inspectors spent 4 hours filling out:
- California AES (Annual Egress & Systems) forms
- Manual data entry
- Copy/paste from inspection notes
- Prone to errors

**Pain level:** Extreme. 4 hours per inspection = 40+ hours per week wasted on paperwork.

### High-Frequency Repetition ‚úÖ

- 20+ inspections per week
- 80+ inspections per month
- Every single inspection required the same 4-hour paperwork process

### Compliance-Critical ‚úÖ

- California state law requires AES forms
- Can't bill client without completed forms
- Late forms = fines and lost revenue

### Terrible Existing Software ‚úÖ

They tried:
- Generic inspection software ($400/user/month = $2,000/month for 5 users)
- Didn't support California AES forms specifically
- Required too many manual steps
- Slower than doing it on paper

**Result:** Gave up and went back to manual Word docs.

---

## The Value Calculation

**Time per inspection:** 4 hours of paperwork
**Frequency:** 20 inspections per week = 80/month
**Hourly cost:** $50 (admin/inspector time)

**Monthly waste:** 4 hours √ó 80 inspections √ó $50 = $16,000/month
**Annual waste:** $192,000/year

**Alternative calculation:**
They would've had to hire a full-time admin at $50K/year to handle the paperwork.

Either way: **Huge pain. Clear value.**

---

## The Solution

Custom software (called "Blaze Operations") that:
- Pulls inspection data from their existing CRM
- Auto-fills California AES forms specifically
- Generates PDFs ready to submit
- Takes 15 minutes instead of 4 hours

**Tech stack:**
- React frontend
- Supabase backend
- Vercel hosting
- Built entirely with Claude Code

**Build time:** 2 weeks (with iterations)

---

## The Pricing

**Upfront:** $15,000
**Monthly:** $1,500/month (hosting, support, updates)

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked:

‚úÖ **Specific solution** - Not "inspection software," but "California AES automation"
‚úÖ **Clear ROI** - Math was simple and undeniable
‚úÖ **Fast build** - 2 weeks from contract to v1
‚úÖ **Their workflow** - Integrated with their existing CRM
‚úÖ **Recurring revenue** - Monthly retainer = predictable income

### What Was Hard:

‚ùå **Scope creep** - They wanted extra features mid-build (had to say "Phase 2")
‚ùå **Bugs** - First version had issues, required quick fixes
‚ùå **Expectation management** - They wanted it yesterday

### What Would Be Done Differently:

- Set clearer timeline expectations upfront
- Include more buffer in estimates
- Phase 2 roadmap in initial proposal
- Weekly check-ins during build
